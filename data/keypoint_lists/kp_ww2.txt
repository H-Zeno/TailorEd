Topic: Political Alliances and Events in world war 2

Keypoints:

1. The war featured two major alliances: the Axis (Germany, Italy, and Japan) and the Allies (primarily the United Kingdom, Soviet Union, and the United States)
2. The Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact was a non-aggression treaty between Nazi Germany and the Soviet Union included secret protocols for dividing Eastern Europe and was a critical prelude to the war
3. Initially neutral, the U.S. entered the war after Japan's attack on Pearl Harbor in 1941, significantly shifting the balance of power
4. Fascism and Nazism drove the Axis agenda, while the Allies were motivated by a need to stop aggressive expansion and genocide
5. Near the war's end, the formation of the United Nations in 1945 aimed to prevent such conflicts in the future.
